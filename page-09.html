SECTION 04 · ADOPTION RISK REGISTER

Seven risks.
All mitigated
architecturally.

Adoption risks are not HR problems. They are architectural failure modes. Each risk below has a technical mitigation, a cultural or process mitigation, and — where relevant — an explicit rollback trigger that makes an unsafe steady-state structurally impossible to sustain undetected.

R-01
HITL Bypass Risk
Critical

Procurement and quality staff routing around the HITL approval interface to meet operational pressure. Risk compounds under quarter-end and regulatory audit cycles.

Mitigation

Technical: No SAP write operation completes without a valid HITL JWT token — the bypass path does not exist at the database layer.

Cultural: PI retrospective reviews of all bypass attempts. Executive sponsor escalation path with mandatory reporting to QMR.

Break-Glass / Emergency Override

Documented emergency override procedure exists for production-line stoppage or imminent patient-safety events. Override requires dual authorisation (VP Supply Chain + QMR), generates an immutable audit record, and triggers a mandatory root-cause review within 48 hours. Override path is covered in Track A Module 5.

R-02
SHAP Literacy Gap
Critical

Approvers rubber-stamping HITL decisions without engaging with SHAP explanations. EU AI Act Article 14 requires meaningful human oversight — a click is not oversight.

Mitigation

Technical: HITL interface requires approver to identify the top contributing feature before the Approve button activates. Confirmation is part of the form — it cannot be bypassed.

Audit: Random quarterly audit of HITL decisions by ML Engineer. Rubber-stamp patterns trigger retraining under Track A Module 2.

Rollback Trigger

If rubber-stamp rate (approvals with <5s review time) exceeds 20% in any 30-day window, module enters supervised mode: ML Engineer co-signs all approvals until root cause is resolved.

R-03
A2A Supplier Adoption Lag
High

Pre-qualified suppliers must deploy or integrate with the A2A Commerce protocol. Adoption lag in the supply base creates a two-speed system that undermines H3 targets. Suppliers in certain jurisdictions may have data sovereignty constraints that block cross-border agent data exchange.

Mitigation

Programme: A2A onboarding programme for top 20 strategic suppliers launched in H3. Dedicated integration support and protocol documentation provided. Supplier data sovereignty reviewed during onboarding — non-EU suppliers assessed under SCCs.

Fallback: Manual PO preserved for suppliers not yet A2A-enabled, tracked as an explicit adoption metric in monthly ops review.

Rollback Trigger

If A2A adoption rate <40% of top-20 suppliers by end H3, programme steering committee reviews whether H3 KPI target is revised or commercial incentives are introduced.

R-04
GDPR Art. 17 vs Immutable Audit Log Tension
High

EU AI Act Article 12 mandates immutable audit logs for high-risk AI systems. GDPR Article 17 grants data subjects the right to erasure. Where supplier personnel data appears in an audit log, these obligations conflict. This is not a theoretical risk — it is a known EU regulatory tension with no settled resolution.

Mitigation

Architecture: Audit logs pseudonymised at source — personal identifiers replaced by opaque tokens before log write. Token-to-identity mapping held separately under DPO governance with defined retention policy.

Legal: DPO documents the legitimate interest / regulatory obligation basis for retaining pseudonymised logs. DPIA completed pre-H1 cutover. External legal opinion obtained on Art. 17 vs Art. 12 boundary.

Escalation Path

Any data subject erasure request touching an audit log record is escalated to DPO + CCO within 24 hours. Response within the 30-day GDPR window is tracked as a compliance KPI.

R-05
Model Confidence Miscalibration / Hallucination
High

A high-confidence wrong recommendation from DemandIQ or SupplierSentinel — particularly in the first 90 days of deployment — erodes approver trust faster than any training programme can restore it. Trust loss leads to rubber-stamping or HITL bypass, compounding the risk.

Mitigation

Technical: Calibration metrics (ECE, reliability diagrams) tracked on all models from day one. Confidence thresholds set conservatively for H2 go-live — high-confidence threshold raised incrementally as calibration data accumulates.

Process: Shadow mode operation for 30 days pre-go-live. Prediction log made available to approvers so they can compare AI recommendation vs outcome retrospectively.

Rollback Trigger

If any model's ECE exceeds 0.15 or calibration drift is detected via Vertex AI Model Monitoring, model is suspended from HITL queue and returns to shadow mode pending retraining.

R-06
SAP Master Data Quality
Medium

Fragmented supplier master data across 4 Ariba instances degrades DemandIQ and SupplierSentinel model performance before training even begins.

Mitigation

Prerequisite gate: Supplier master harmonisation is an H1 prerequisite — DemandIQ training is gated on master data quality score ≥85%.

Ongoing: Data quality dashboard with MDM team ownership. Score published weekly to programme steering committee.

Rollback Trigger

If master data quality score falls below 80% post-go-live, DemandIQ confidence thresholds are tightened automatically — model operates in conservative mode until score recovers.

R-07
EAA Accessibility Non-Compliance (HITL Interface)
Medium

The European Accessibility Act (EAA) applies to enterprise software used by employees in the EU from June 2025. The HITL interface is a mission-critical enterprise tool deployed to approvers across 22 countries. WCAG 2.1 AA compliance is a legal obligation, not an option.

Mitigation

Design: HITL interface built to WCAG 2.1 AA from the first sprint. Accessibility audit contracted before H2 go-live — remediation items treated as critical defects in the release backlog.

Ongoing: Automated accessibility testing (axe-core or equivalent) integrated into CI/CD pipeline. Accessibility regression is a build failure condition.

Gate Condition

No HITL interface release passes H2 go-live gate without a signed external accessibility audit report. CISO and CCO co-sign. EAA compliance is a launch blocker — not a post-launch remediation item.

Every design decision across these nine pages traces to a named GCP service, a TOGAF phase, a regulatory obligation, or a real supply chain pain point. Nothing is decorative. Everything is buildable. The architecture is production-grade — not a concept document dressed in technical language. TOGAF ADM Phases A–F are covered across the portfolio; Phases G (Implementation Governance) and H (Architecture Change Management) are represented in this adoption page's KPI framework, RACI, and risk register.

The A2A Commerce pattern — a supplier agent negotiating and transacting with a procurement agent across organisational boundaries — does not appear in any other public supply chain AI portfolio. It is original architectural thinking, expressed as a concrete implementation against a named GCP service stack, with a defined fallback, a supplier onboarding programme, and an adoption KPI with a baseline.
